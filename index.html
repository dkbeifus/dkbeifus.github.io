Daniel Beifus

Daniel Beifus has been testing software for the past 13 years without a bachelor's degree. He got his start in this field by testing the system he used to operate in the US Navy. The degree is not required, but after a recent lay off it has become apparent that it can be a hinderance in the hiring process. With that in mind, he is completing a degree he started some years prior.

Goals for the course

The most obvious goal for this course is to receive a passing grade and advance towards degree completion, but that is not the only goal here. The courses for a degree are planned out to give the students the knowledge and skills to succeed in a career. A major goal for this course is to glean additional skills and thought processes to order to increase the available skills in his skillset of software development/testing.

Goals for career

While every job has the inherent goal of earning money to make functioning in society possible, jobs also add a sense of accomplish. Daniel's professional goal is to work with software development which feels too much like a hobby for it to feel like work. He enjoys seeing how systems break which leads to improvements which ultimately make them harder to break. That gives a sense of accomplishment. As far as long term career ambitions, a lot of people see working their way up into management is the pinnacle of succes but Daniel would rather become a subject matter expert than do people management.
